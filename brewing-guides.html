Grind Size Matters

The grind size dramatically affects extraction and flavor. Coarse for French press, medium for drip, fine for espresso.

  • Extra Coarse: Cold brew, cowboy coffee
  • Coarse: French press, percolator
  • Medium-Coarse: Chemex, pour-over
  • Medium: Drip coffee makers
  • Medium-Fine: V60, Aeropress
  • Fine: Espresso, Moka pot

Water Temperature

Water temperature affects extraction rate and flavor balance. Too hot burns, too cool under-extracts.

  • 195-205¬∞F (90-96¬∞C): Optimal range for most brewing
  • 200¬∞F (93¬∞C): Sweet spot for pour-over
  • 185-195¬∞F (85-90¬∞C): Light roasts, delicate flavors
  • 205¬∞F (96¬∞C): Dark roasts, full extraction

Coffee to Water Ratio

The golden ratio ensures consistent strength and flavor. Adjust to your taste preferences.

  • 1:15-1:17: Standard pour-over ratio
  • 1:12-1:15: Stronger, more concentrated
  • 1:17-1:20: Lighter, more subtle
  • Example: 25g coffee to 375g water (1:15)

Brewing Time

Different brewing methods require different contact times between coffee and water.

  • Espresso: 25-30 seconds
  • Pour-over: 2:30-4:00 minutes
  • French Press: 4:00 minutes
  • Cold Brew: 12-24 hours

Perfect Cold Brew

Smooth, rich cold brew coffee that's perfect for hot summer days. Low acidity and naturally sweet flavor that's perfect on its own or with milk.

‚è±Ô∏è 5 min prep üïê 12 hours üë• 4 servings

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup coarsely ground coffee (medium-dark roast)
  • 4 cups cold water
  • Ice cubes
  • Milk or cream (optional)
  • Simple syrup (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Combine coffee grounds and cold water in a large jar or container
  2. Stir well to ensure all grounds are saturated
  3. Cover and refrigerate for 12-24 hours
  4. Strain through fine mesh or coffee filter twice for clarity
  5. Serve over ice, dilute with water or milk as desired

V60 Pour-Over Perfection

Master the Japanese V60 pour-over technique for bright, clean coffee with complex flavor notes. Precision brewing at its finest with detailed timing guide.

‚è±Ô∏è 2 min prep üïê 4 minutes üë• 1 serving

Ingredients:

  • 22g medium-fine ground coffee
  • 350ml water at 200¬∞F (93¬∞C)
  • V60 filter paper
  • Gooseneck kettle recommended

Instructions:

  1. Rinse V60 filter and preheat dripper and cup
  2. Add coffee grounds, create small well in center
  3. Start timer, bloom with 40ml water for 30 seconds
  4. Pour in slow circles: 150ml by 1:15, 250ml by 2:00
  5. Final pour to 350ml by 2:30, finish dripping by 3:30

Iced Vanilla Latte

A refreshing twist on the classic latte with homemade vanilla syrup. Perfect for warm weather and easily customizable to your taste preferences.

‚è±Ô∏è 5 min prep üïê 5 minutes üë• 1 serving

Coffee Cupping Guide

Learn the professional method for tasting and evaluating coffee. Develop your palate and understand flavor notes like a professional barista.

‚è±Ô∏è 10 min prep üïê 20 minutes üë• 3-5 coffees

What You Need:

  • 3-5 different coffee samples (8g each)
  • Cupping bowls or small glasses
  • Hot water (200¬∞F/93¬∞C)
  • Cupping spoons
  • Timer

Instructions:

  1. Grind each coffee to medium-coarse consistency
  2. Smell the dry grounds and note aromas
  3. Add hot water, start 4-minute timer
  4. Break the crust at 4 minutes, smell again
  5. Wait 10-15 minutes, then taste with spoon
  6. Note acidity, body, flavor, and finish

Classic Affogato

The perfect Italian dessert combining hot espresso with cold vanilla gelato. Simple, elegant, and absolutely delicious as an after-dinner treat.

‚è±Ô∏è 2 min prep üïê 3 minutes üë• 1 serving

Ingredients:

  • 1-2 shots hot espresso
  • 2 scoops vanilla gelato or ice cream
  • Amaretti cookies (optional)
  • Dark chocolate shavings (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Place vanilla gelato in a chilled glass or bowl
  2. Brew fresh espresso shots
  3. Pour hot espresso directly over the gelato
  4. Serve immediately with cookies on the side
  5. Optional: top with chocolate shavings
